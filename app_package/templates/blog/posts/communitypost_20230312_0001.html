What Sticks Data Descriptions

Sleep

Is the Oura Ring Sleep Score. The Oura Ring website answers the question how well did you sleep last night? It ranges from 0 to 100.

·       85 or higher: Your sleep is optimal, keep it up!

·       70-84: Your sleep is good, you’re on the right track.

·       Under 70: Your sleep is off and maybe balance seems off and there may be lifestyle changes you can make to improve it.

 

Your sleep score is made up of seven different personalized sleep elements known as Sleep Contributors. Your contributors help you answer deeper questions about what is impacting the overall quality of your sleep.

·       Total Sleep: “Did you get enough sleep?”

·       Efficiency: “How much of your time in bed did you actually spend sleeping?”

·       Restfulness: “Did you toss and turn?”

·       REM Sleep: “Did you get enough REM sleep?”

·       Deep Sleep: “Did you get enough deep sleep?”

·       Latency: “How quickly did you fall asleep?”

·       Timing: “When are you getting to sleep? Are you going to bed according to your body’s natural circadian rhythm?”

 

Source: https://ouraring.com/blog/sleep-score/

 

What date does the sleep score apply towards?

What Sticks chart shows the sleep score corresponding to that evening’s sleep. The score in the charts corresponds to the start date of the sleep session. Therefore, when you’re looking at the dashboard chart the sleep score applies to the sleep that took place the evening of the corresponding date.

 

The Oura application shows the sleep score corresponding to the day one wakes up.

 

Average Temperature

This is the average daily temperature based on your location. This measure is reported by Visual Crossing and you can find more information from their website here: https://www.visualcrossing.com/weather-history

 

WS users can enter their location from the accounts page. Once the location is set WS will call the weather API to retrieve the past day’s average temperature. If you go somewhere else just update the location and WS will call the temperature from that new location

 

Cloud Cover

This is a measure of how much of the sky is covered by clouds. This measure ranges between 0 and 100% where 100% indicates that the sky is completely obscured by clouds.

 

This data is collected by VisualCrossing.com. You can find out more information about the cloud cover measure here.

Source: https://www.visualcrossing.com/resources/documentation/weather-data/where-can-i-find-historical-cloud-and-visibility-data/

 

Like the weather data WS users can enter their location and the cloud cover data gets updated daily. If you move to a different location just update your location.
