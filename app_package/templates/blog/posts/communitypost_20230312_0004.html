What Sticks Analyzes Your Steps!

 

What impacts the amount of walking I do most?

 

Between average temperature, cloud cover, and quality of sleep it seems when its less cloudy I tend to walk more.

 

Fig 1. What Sticks Steps Dashboard

 

Here is a picture of my What Sticks steps dashboard. It shows the correlation between various data collected by my phone with the number of steps I take each day.

 

The average daily temperature is the temperature in my region this is collected using my location data. As one might expect the correlation is positive so when its warmer outside, I tend to walk more.

 

The cloud cover is an index that determines how cloudy it is outside. Using my location what sticks collects this daily. This is metric also seems to follow intuition because it suggests that on cloudy days I tend to walk less.

 

The sleep metric is my Oura sleep score. It shows a positive correlation to the sleep score so when I have better sleep, I walk more.

 

What does this tell me?

You might be thinking to yourself “nothing new here.” And to some extent you’re right. I think if most of us had to guess that when its warmer, sunny and I slept well, I walked more.

 

To this I have three things to say:

1.     Evidence: now we have some evidence to back these hypothesis

2.     Magnitude: I can see which one is truly more impactful

3.     As the dataset gets larger and more variables get added I’ll start to get a fuller picture of what truly impacts my life.

 

 

Fig 2. Plot of Steps, Sleep, Cloud Cover and Average Temperature

 

 

Right now, there is a lot of information and most of it is based on hypothesis and anecdotal evidence. What Sticks can give you personalized feedback on data already being collected.

 

In the past I have used a variety of applications that have all been really great at telling me what is going on what my body. They measuring things very well. Now Oura ring is remarkable at passive data collection. Without me ever having to enter data it knows when I go to sleep wake up and it measures my sleep quality. Even though I sort of know when I sleep well or not now Oura makes this definitive.

 

While this is great, how do I use it?

 

This is where What Sticks comes in. All these tools are great for measuring things that are going on in my life, but they are not answering why they are happening. Furthermore, the reason things are happening in my life are likely to differ from other people’s. So even if there is a great clinical study released with conclusive results, they are not conclusive for me. In fact, I know some people tend to be skeptical if things do not align with their preconceptions.

 

While I cannot argue that What Sticks is the definitive conclusion. I can already hear my analytical friends calling out “Correlation is not causation.” Yes, this is true and by no means am I saying this is the final answer, but what we do have here is a data point. I would argue one of the better data points you have.
